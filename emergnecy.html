Emergency Procedures

How to respond quickly, calmly, and legally to fire, medical, weather, and behavioral emergencies.

Last Reviewed: August 30, 2025

1. Fire

  • Pull the fire alarm and call 911
  • Evacuate residents using front and back exits
  • Meet across the street at the designated safe point (Gichitwaa Kateri Church)
  • Account for all residents and staff
  • Do not re-enter the home until cleared by fire officials

2. Medical Emergency

  • Call 911 immediately if the person is unresponsive or in distress
  • Provide CPR or first aid only if trained and delegated
  • Call the on-call nurse and director after 911
  • Document what occurred in RTasks and complete an incident report

3. Severe Weather

  • Move residents to the basement level during tornado warnings
  • Keep emergency supplies (flashlights, water, radio) on hand
  • Remain indoors during blizzards or storms
  • Notify the nurse if any resident appears distressed

4. Elopement or Missing Resident

  • Call 911 if a resident leaves without permission and is vulnerable
  • Immediately notify the director and nurse
  • Record time last seen, clothing, and direction
  • Document everything in RTasks

5. Behavioral Emergency

  • If behavior poses danger, call 911 first
  • Then notify nurse and director
  • Do not attempt physical restraint unless delegated
  • Redirect if possible and ensure safety of others
  • Log behavior note in RTasks immediately after

6. Power Outage

  • Use emergency flashlights (do not use candles)
  • If outage exceeds 2 hours, contact nurse and director
  • Check if resident medical equipment is battery-powered (e.g., CPAP)

7. Evacuation Supplies

Supplies should be kept near main exit and updated monthly:

  • First aid kit
  • Flashlights and batteries
  • Blankets and gloves
  • Water and non-perishable snacks
  • Resident contact list and MARs
